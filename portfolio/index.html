Shooty Game

This is one of the first games that I tried to write. I spent most of 9th grade writing this. It is buggy, laggy, and ugly, but it is one of my favorite projects. I abandoned this version of the project for a version with different game mechanics, and that could be scaled to multiplayer.

The main page is "popup.html" rather than "index.html", because this was origonally made as a chrome extension. At this time, the extension is not available for download.

Maze Game

This is my first game. In 8th grade, I was interested in maze generation. By myself, I developed two algorithms for generating mazes, which I later learned were called Kruskal's algorithm and Prim's algorithm. Learning the names of the algorithms that I had "invented" was a little saddening, but exciting at the same time. This is actually how I was introduced to graph theory.

The main page is "popup.html" rather than "index.html", because this was origonally made as a chrome extension. At this time, the extension is not available for download.

lightBulb

This was my first attempt at having a webpage communicate over the web. If you click the lightbulb, it will change for everyone who has the webpage open, not just you. To accomplish this <sarcasm>monumental</sarcasm> feat, it took me a week of searching the internet, and and another week of learning about AJAX requests and PHP. It took me so long because I didn't have any idea what I was doing (and I am self-taught - I've never had a more confused teacher).

Websocket Cursor Tracking

This was my second attempt at having a webpage communicate over the web, which I made to learn how websockets work. This front-end application draws a square where your cursor is. Actually, it draws squares where everone's cursors are. You can test this by opening the page in multiple tabs or on another computer.
